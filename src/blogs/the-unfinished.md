When we talk about motherhood, we cover everything - pregnancy, postpartum, mental load, newborn routines. These things come naturally. But somewhere between all of that, something quieter happens. Something every mother feels but nobody really names.

We start things and can't finish them. Every single day.

As mothers, we enter the world of “The Unfinished”.

You are planning to work on something for a while, you finally find a window - worked around nap schedules, meal times, bedtime routines, early mornings, late nights - just to get here. Your mind is working at its peak. You start. And that peak focus doesn't come easy. You know that.

And then they need you. Not because of something dramatic. Just a cry. A call. A small human who needs you right now, always right now, never five minutes from now.

And you go. You always go. Because they are yours, and that will always matter more.

You come back to a cold cup of coffee and a mind that's completely reset. The half-written email. The meal gone cold. The thought you were this close to figuring out - just sitting there, waiting. You realize, the window is closed. You're left mid-thought, mid-task, mid-everything - and there's no clean place to set any of it down. You can't explain it to anyone. You just carry it forward into the next thing.

It's not just the big things. It's the shower you cut short. The sentence you never finished saying. You move from one half-done thing to the next, all day. And at the end of it - nothing feels complete. Not the work, not the chores. Not even you. Just a long list of things left somewhere in between, and a tiredness that sleep doesn't fix.

That's when something snaps inside. Not the kind you can express - the kind you feel and suppress. Because your heart knows it's not a one-time event. It's a recurring one, for years you can't yet count. You're not frustrated about today. You're frustrated about the pattern. The one with no end date, no finish line. And somewhere along the way you stop expecting uninterrupted time - which feels like a loss of its own kind.

It accumulates. Quietly. Until one day something small happens and the untamed chaos of it all just spills. The mental weight it unleashes - sometimes it gets heavy. Not because of them. Never because of them. Just because of everything you quietly carry alongside them.

You can love someone completely, have them be your whole world, and still miss the version of you that got to just... finish things. 
Those two things live in you at the same time. That's the part that's hard to say out loud. And one can never really understand this if they haven't lived it - the daily dance of deep adoration and overwhelming exasperation.

That's motherhood. Not broken, not wrong. Just unfinished - in every sense of the word.
