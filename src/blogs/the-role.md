There are things which one cannot understand fully until they experience it on their own. The joy of priceless moments and the brutal reality of it - motherhood taught me both. It's rewarding, it's fulfilling, and at the same time it's draining, it's challenging. The acceptance of it is the first step - when we fully understand it, we give it the value it truly deserves.

For the longest time, this role was so under-appreciated that the people doing it were made to believe that it's normal, that this is what it's supposed to be. But being a mother in today's time gave me a very clear reality of it. The realization - it's not a role; it's the role.

It's not something we can mess up and recover from. It's the foundation of a life that we have created. The role is huge - with higher expectations and responsibilities, and no chance of error. It's the vital role in shaping the future generation. That's the big picture and the hard reality. And hence, the pressure.

Whether we do it with a village or do it alone, we feel the load of it. The constant clock of a mother's brain never really stops.

Modern day parenting is very different from earlier parenting. Though a few concepts remain the same, the wider perspective has shifted. The main reason - we are parenting in a completely different world, and the approach needs to change. With all of this, modern day moms find themselves at the center of it all. They are working hard at building something for themselves - a path, an identity - and they are equally invested in their child's life, refusing to miss a beat of their childhood. From the outside, it looks very inspirational - we are creating an image our child can look up to - but at the same time, it's draining them to the core.

Being the couple while being parents, thriving at work, and working towards maintaining a healthy lifestyle - all while parenting. It feels overwhelming, and sometimes it is. There are moments when I found myself wondering - is all this in my mind? How am I supposed to do it all and feel like it's normal? Many times, the mind fights its own battle between I can't do this and I need to do this. For us, for them. We owe it to them - we are the ones who brought them into this world and we are responsible for all of it. That's the part we hold on to, and everything works around it. As a mother, I find it hard - not because I'm an overthinker, but because I accept the truth of it. And I am proud of this version of myself.

The other side of this role - the part that keeps me going - is the joy in every little thing. The cuddles, the giggles, every first. Those tiny hands that melt my heart when they reach for my face. Those sparkly eyes that see their whole world in me. It's a beautiful feeling, beyond words. It's a privilege to play a part in raising someone who is meant for greater things, who will lead a life on their own terms and live it to the fullest while doing so.
